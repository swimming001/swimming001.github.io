A Sad Day (But a Step Forward)

呜呜呜，A Lesson Carved in Code 😢

Today, the unexpected happened, and it honestly feels like the saddest event. I want to document this moment so I can remember it forever, learn from it, and look back on how much I’ve grown.

Let me break down what happened simply.

The Bug That Blinded Me 🔍

I walked into my Java-2 Exam feeling confident. But code has a strange way of humbling you. I completely overlooked a critical detail: the price could be negative. Because the exam requirements were bunched together in a single, continuous line of text, debugging it during the test felt nearly impossible. The clue was deeply connected, looking something like this:
"...the price, stock > 0..."

My eyes immediately locked onto stock > 0, but I completely missed the conditional logic required for price. I spent so much time spiraling, trying to figure out if the issue was a negative value or an incorrect logical operation. I felt so frustrated and crazy that I couldn’t even see the obvious truth right in front of me.

In the end, that tiny oversight cost me 2 points.

The ACMer’s Pride ⚔️

It hurts. It really makes me disappointed because I hold myself to the standard of an OI/ACM competitive programmer. I felt like I should have achieved absolute perfection, but I couldn’t bring home that full pride this time. 555 (Sobbing)… 😭

💡 My Take-Aways

Instead of just being sad, I want to lock these three lessons into my mental repo:

  1. The Compounding Power of Small Details 🪵
    A single tiny oversight forces you to question every single test case. From now on, I will analyze problem statements with absolute, meticulous thoroughness. No word gets left behind.
  2. Humility Before the Matrix 🐿️
    Sometimes, you need to realize how small you are and how massive the world is. Right now, I feel like a tiny squirrel or a small bug, peering out into an infinite universe with wide, small eyes. There is always more to learn.
  3. The Grind Never Stops 🚀
    I will work twice as hard to sharpen my skills. I will train myself to think deeply, analyze the finest details, and expand my domain knowledge so nothing catches me off guard again.

“A bug caught in an exam is a lesson learned for a lifetime.”
Next time, the compiler won’t win. 💪
